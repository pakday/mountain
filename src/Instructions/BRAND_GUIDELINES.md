## What Our Brand Stands For

**Core Statement:** Strengthening the community through local advocacy, education, partnership, & promotion of local businesses

### Mission Pillars

#### 1. Local Advocacy

- **Responsibility:** Work with city and local governments to advocate for local businesses
- **Goal:** Ensure local business interests are represented in policy decisions

#### 2. Business Partnerships

- **Responsibility:** Connect business owners to build strategic partnerships for success
- **Goal:** Create valuable connections and networking opportunities for members

#### 3. Business Education

- **Responsibility:** Host ongoing learning experiences and provide business resources to members
- **Goal:** Support member growth through continuous education and resources

#### 4. Promotion of Local Businesses

- **Responsibility:** Use a mix of marketing methods to increase exposure and promotion of members
- **Goal:** Amplify the visibility and success of local businesses

## Color Palette

### Primary Color

- **Color:** Deep Blue
- **Hex Code:** `#0D3B66`
- **Usage:** Primary brand color for main elements, headers, and dominant design components

### Secondary Colors

- **Light Gray**
  - **Hex Code:** `#EDEDED`
  - **Usage:** Backgrounds and subtle supporting elements
- **Slate Blue**
  - **Hex Code:** `#3F6183`
  - **Usage:** Secondary supporting elements and accents

### Accent Colors

Use only the core accent colors below. Additional palette colors are not part of the MWCC brand system.

| Color Name | Hex Code  | Primary Use                                 |
| ---------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| Teal       | `#5DCAD1` | Interactive elements, links, icons          |
| Yellow     | `#F7DE32` | CTA highlights, badges, high-impact accents |
| Off-White  | `#FFFAF9` | Premium backgrounds, soft surface color     |

### Color Usage Rules

- Use only the six brand colors in visual design and page styles.
- Avoid additional Tailwind palettes such as `slate-*`, `gray-*`, `neutral-*`, `stone-*`, and `zinc-*`.
- Use only these alpha levels: `/10`, `/20`, `/40`, `/60`, `/70`, `/80`, `/90`.

## Iconography & Visual Treatments

### Program Icons System

The following icons represent key Chamber programs and should be used consistently across all communications:

#### Core Program Icons (8 Total)

1. **Member Spotlights**
   - Highlights individual member success stories
   - Used in: Social media, newsletters, website features

2. **Ribbon Cuttings**
   - Celebrates grand openings and business launches
   - Used in: Social media, event announcements, flyers

3. **Teacher Appreciation**
   - Recognizes and honors local educators
   - Used in: Event materials, promotional campaigns

4. **Connect + Learn**
   - Represents educational programs and networking events
   - Used in: Luncheon announcements, educational content

5. **Women in Business**
   - Focuses on women entrepreneurs and leaders
   - Used in: Program-specific communications

6. **Business of the Month**
   - Monthly business recognition program
   - Used in: Social media, website features, newsletters

7. **Digital Coverage**
   - Online content, digital/social media sharing
   - Used in: Blog posts, digital announcements, online platforms

8. **Member Passport Program**
   - Loyalty/engagement program for members
   - Used in: Program materials, engagement campaigns

### Icon Design Principles

**A Fresh New Look:** Adding repetitive cues to designate standard programs brings consistency to the brand.

### Icon Usage Guidelines

#### Icons with Solid Backgrounds

- **Color Rule:** Use white or primary color (`#0D3B66`)
- **Best For:** Contained, branded spaces (buttons, cards, badges)
- **Purpose:** Creates strong visual hierarchy and emphasis

#### Solitary Icons (No Confined Background)

- **Color Rule:** Use accent color palette
- **Best For:** Inline elements, decorative purposes, flexible layouts
- **Purpose:** Maintains visual flexibility while staying on-brand

## Brand Voice & Communications Strategy

### How We Speak to Members & Residents

#### Brand Perception

- **Desired Perception:** A modern influencer for successful businesses
- **Tone:** Professional yet approachable
- **Authority:** Industry-knowledgeable and inspiring

#### Communications Strategy

- **Approach:** Digital-first strategy
- **Reach:** Meet members and residents where they find information
- **Channels:** Primary focus on digital platforms and social media
- **Goal:** Consistent, accessible communication across all touchpoints

#### Writing Style

##### Characteristics

- **Tone:** Fun and whimsical with professional overarching tone
- **Voice:** Encouraging, accessible, business-forward
- **Style:** Modern, engaging, authentic

##### Key Principles

1. Maintain professional credibility
2. Inject personality and warmth
3. Use conversational language
4. Balance enthusiasm with authority
5. Be clear and concise

## Implementation Guidelines

### Consistency Recommendations

1. **Always use the primary color** (`#0D3B66`) for main brand touchpoints
2. **Pair secondary colors** with primary for balanced designs
3. **Use accent colors strategically** to differentiate programs
4. **Maintain typography hierarchy** by using Roboto Slab for headlines
5. **Apply icon system consistently** across all program communications
6. **Follow brand voice guidelines** in all written communications
7. **Reference template library** for standard communications

### When to Use Each Element

| Touchpoint        | Primary Color | Secondary Color | Icon | Font             |
| ----------------- | ------------- | --------------- | ---- | ---------------- |
| Logo/Headers      | Yes           | No              | Yes  | Roboto Slab      |
| Backgrounds       | Maybe         | Yes             | No   | Montserrat       |
| Call-to-Actions   | Accent        | No              | Yes  | Roboto Condensed |
| Body Text         | Minimal       | No              | No   | Roboto Condensed |
| Program Materials | Yes           | Yes             | Yes  | All three        |
